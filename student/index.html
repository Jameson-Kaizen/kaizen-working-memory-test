What's Your Study Memory?

This measures your working memory, the mental scratchpad you use to hold and juggle information for a few seconds. It is what lets you follow a lesson, keep your place in a math problem, and remember what was just said.

Four quick rounds, about 6 minutes. You get a practice try before each one.

  • Numbers forward and backward holding words and numbers.
  • Spatial pattern remembering where things are.
  • Updating keeping track as things change fast.
To get an accurate read:
  • Use a quiet moment with no distractions.
  • Do not write anything down or say it out loud.
  • Watch closely. Each item disappears before you answer.
  • Go with your gut and keep a steady pace.

An informal estimate for your own insight, not a clinical or diagnostic assessment.
